# Mock Interview Formats

Defines the structure for each interview stage simulation.

## Phone Screen (30 minutes)

- **Questions:** 3-4 behavioral/motivational
- **Pacing:** 5-7 minutes per question, 2-3 minutes for user questions at end
- **Persona:** Recruiter (default) or Engineering Manager
- **Follow-ups:** 1 per question max
- **Focus:** Communication clarity, motivation, culture fit

**Question mix:**
- 1 motivation/career question
- 1-2 behavioral (collaboration, ownership)
- 1 situational (optional, time permitting)

## Technical Round (45 minutes)

- **Questions:** 1-2 coding + 1 technical discussion
- **Pacing:** 20-25 minutes per coding question, 10-15 minutes for discussion
- **Persona:** Staff Engineer
- **Follow-ups:** Deep dive into approach and tradeoffs
- **Focus:** Problem-solving, technical communication, code quality

**Question mix:**
- 1-2 from technical.yaml (code quality, operations)
- Discussion of approach and tradeoffs after each

## Onsite Behavioral (45 minutes)

- **Questions:** 4-5 behavioral
- **Pacing:** 7-10 minutes per question including follow-ups
- **Persona:** Engineering Manager or Bar Raiser
- **Follow-ups:** 1-2 per question
- **Focus:** Depth, specificity, pattern of impact

**Question mix:**
- 2 behavioral (varied subcategories)
- 1 leadership
- 1 situational
- 1 from weakest category (based on session history)

**Question selection:**
- 1 from system-design.yaml
- Follow-ups are driven by the candidate's design choices, not pre-scripted

## Bar Raiser (60 minutes)

- **Questions:** 4-6 behavioral, multi-angle
- **Pacing:** 8-12 minutes per question including deep follow-ups
- **Persona:** Bar Raiser
- **Follow-ups:** 2-3 per question, probing for consistency
- **Focus:** Judgment, ownership, authenticity, customer impact

**Question mix:**
- 2 behavioral (ownership, failure/growth)
- 1 leadership (decision-making)
- 1 situational (ethics or conflict)
- 1-2 revisiting themes from earlier questions at a different angle
